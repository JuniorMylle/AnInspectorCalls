Eric

An Inspector Calls
Erics relationship with the other characters

Eric is clearly presented to be a character with many secrets. He is uncomfortable with the environment in which he resides in. He is always intimidated and interrupted by his father who treats him as an inferior child. Eric has a drinking problem, he gets drunk and acts in a very strange manner during the interrogation of the inspector.

Who is Eric

In act one before the inspector came in Eric acts like a child and is not very serious. He is also a rebel, and tries to take on Mr Birlings selfish views who always interrupts him or not agree with him. He changes character in act two by becoming stronger and telling his dad the truth.

What do the audience think of Eric

Priestley presents Eric like a child in act one but he changed in act two. First in act one Eric was a weak person that was always beat by his father but he seems to have a bit of character, sometimes he refuses with his father (Mr. Birling) beats him down. Priestley shows that Eric changes between in act one and act two because he shows the audience that the children are the future, that they have the ability to change.
